Paper Title

Switchless Multiplexing of Graphene Active Sensor Arrays for Brain Mapping

1. [Summary]

This article present a new approach that use frequency-division multiplexing (FDM) of graphene solution-gated field-effect-transistors (g-SGFETs) in order to eliminate the need for onsite switches and to reduce the fabrication complexity of high-count neural probes.

The article first introduced some basic concepts and how FDM system works. Then, it analyzed the In-vitro characterization of g-SGFETs. Next, author discussed the scalability of g-SGFET arrays multiplexed in the frequency-domain. At last, the article showed the in-vivo evaluation result of FDM graphene neural probes.

2. [Strengths]

  • The problem of high complexity front end circuit stated by the article is a common and significant problem in multi-channel neural interface design.

  • Good characterization and performance in some aspects:

    • Reduce the fabrication complexity
    • sensitivity of g-SGFETs does not differ dramatically in both DC and AM modes, showing only slight changes in its ùëâùëîùë† dependence.
    • higher mobility and a smaller interface capacitance compared with other active sensors
    • good capability for the recording of infra-slow neural activity with a high spatial resolution
    • biocompatibility of graphene

  • There is sufficient experiments to show good characterization and scalability of the system

  • The FDM solution is easy to understand and realize.
  • Several Schemes are given to improve scalability
    • A scheme of reducing resistance is given to reduce crosstalk
    • using Digital filters and orthogonal modulation
    • Adjust the phase of carrier to minimize the amplitude of the carriers superposition

3. [Weaknesses]

  • The order of the pictures is not quite right, Figure3 is behind Figure4
  • Several mistakes. For example, missing fc3 in Figure4a
  • g-SGFETs is not small enough
  • Maximum carrier frequency is 600kHz and each sensor takes tens of kHz bandwidth(Digital filters and orthogonal modulation are needed). Thar means it can only support a few number of carrier.
  • No enough comparison with other probes
  • Analysis lack of standards. There needs to be a standard by which to evaluate the results of experiments
  • The in-vivo only test 4x8 array, not too much channel

4. [Overall rating]

  • week accept

5. [Question]

  • How to get that many carriers? carrier-generating circuits can become very complicated. Can you show your carrier-generating solution.

  • Besides oscillator, FDM need a lot of filters and synchronizers. This part of circuit is much more complex than switches in TDM. So, why use FDM?

  • Can you give a comparison in size, channel number and other aspects between your probe and other solutions.

  • The g-SGFETs is not small enough, it seems that this scheme cannot achieve many channels
